Rabies

What is rabies?

Rabies is a contagious and fatal disease that is passed from animal to human through bite. It is mostly transmitted from dogs and bats and mostly occurs at low income countries. Rabies is also caused by the rabies virus.

When was it discovered?

Rabies was first dscovered in Italy by a physician named Girolamo Fracastoro in the 16th century. The cure was finally found in 1885 by a French biologist called Louis Pasteur.

How is it transmitted?

Rabies is transmitted when an animal bites another animal or human and spreads the rabies virus through the bite. In some cases it can spread when the infected saliva gets into an open wound. The virus travels from the open wound and into the brain causing inflammation.

What is incubation period?

Incubation period is the time between the bite and the appearance of symptoms. Incubation period for rabies can vary. Typically it would be three to eight weeks, but can be shorter or longer in some cases.

What are the early symptoms?

  • Seizures (convulsions)
  • Muscle spasms and unusual postures.
  • Weakness or paralysis.
  • Confusion, bizarre or strange thoughts, or hallucinations.
  • Irritability or agressiveness.
  • Excessive movements or agitation.

What are the later symptoms?

  • Nausea.
  • Anxiety.
  • Confusion.
  • Fever.
  • Vomiting.
  • Hyperactivity.
  • Agitation.
